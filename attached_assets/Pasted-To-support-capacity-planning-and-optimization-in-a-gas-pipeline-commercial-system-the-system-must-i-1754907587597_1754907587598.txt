To support capacity planning and optimization in a gas pipeline commercial system, the system must integrate operational data, commercial rules, and market requirements to efficiently manage the available pipeline capacity. Below is a structured breakdown of key components and functionalities your system should provide:
________________________________________
🔧 Functional Capabilities
1. Capacity Modeling & Forecasting
•	Define pipeline segments, compressor stations, delivery/receipt points, and constraints.
•	Support for static and dynamic capacity profiles (e.g., seasonal variations).
•	Historical flow data and nominations to predict future usage patterns.
•	Integration with hydraulic simulation tools (e.g., Synergi Gas, Pipeline Studio) for physical capacity validation.
2. Shipper Contractual Rights Tracking
•	Maintain long-term transportation agreements (FT – Firm Transport, IT – Interruptible Transport).
•	Track firm vs. interruptible capacity rights, including primary and secondary market transactions.
•	Allocate contracted capacity by path, segment, and point.
3. Nomination and Scheduling Integration
•	Use daily and intra-day nominations to monitor real-time utilization vs. contracted capacity.
•	Highlight potential over/under-nominations and available headroom.
4. Optimization Engine
•	Apply algorithms (e.g., linear programming or constraint-based logic) to:
o	Maximize throughput
o	Minimize fuel usage/compression costs
o	Balance demand across multiple paths
•	Scenario modeling: simulate outages, maintenance, or new customer demand.
________________________________________
📈 Planning & Reporting Tools
1. What-If Simulation Interface
•	Evaluate impacts of:
o	Adding/removing customers or load
o	Upstream/downstream constraints
o	Compressor derating or pipeline maintenance
2. Capacity Availability Reports
•	Daily/monthly reports for operations and commercial use.
•	Show available vs. contracted vs. actual capacity per segment.
3. Congestion & Constraint Alerts
•	Real-time notifications when capacity thresholds are reached or exceeded.
________________________________________
🔁 Integration Requirements
•	SCADA / Pipeline Operations System: For real-time flow and pressure data.
•	GIS / Asset Management: For topology and infrastructure modeling.
•	Billing & Contract Management: To track shipper entitlements and penalties.
•	Regulatory Reporting: To generate capacity-related compliance reports (e.g., Open Season, CER/FERC reporting).
________________________________________
🧠 Optimization Scenarios
Scenario	Optimization Goal
Peak Day Demand	Maximize firm delivery within constraints
Maintenance Outage	Reallocate flows, reroute interruptible capacity
New Customer Request	Evaluate capacity availability and trigger expansion if needed
Market Rebalancing	Identify underutilized paths for secondary market offerings
________________________________________
🧩 Optional Advanced Features
•	AI/ML Forecasting: Predict future capacity bottlenecks based on consumption and nomination trends.
•	Digital Twin Integration: Real-time comparison of planned vs. actual flow behavior.
•	Capacity Trading Module: Facilitate secondary market capacity trades (shipper-to-shipper).
